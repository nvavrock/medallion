## Era narratives

### 1978–1988: Monemetrics, models, and Medallion launch

Simons moved from Stony Brook chairmanship into markets with a scientist-first hiring instinct [[claim:CLM-2026-001]] [[claim:CLM-2026-010]]. **Leonard Baum** and **James Ax** appear in public timelines as the key model builders before Medallion’s 1988 launch [[claim:CLM-2026-010]] [[claim:CLM-2026-011]] [[claim:CLM-2026-004]]. This era is thinly documented at the trade-ticket level; most detail is retrospective journalism.

### 1989–1993: Crisis, Berlekamp overhaul, employee-only pivot

The 1989 drawdown and Ax departure are central to the “quant discipline beats intuition” story in [@zuckerman2019] and [@wikipedia_renaissance_technologies] [[claim:CLM-2026-005]]. Berlekamp’s six-month engineering sprint and 1990 rebound are among the best-attested performance facts—still **secondary**, not audited public filings [[claim:CLM-2026-006]]. Closing Medallion to outsiders in **1993** is a structural fact for capacity and secrecy narratives [[claim:CLM-2026-007]].

### 1993–2009: IBM hires, equities scaling, Simons CEO era

Brown and Mercer’s **1993** arrival is sometimes mis-remembered as “late 1990s” in casual commentary; public timelines anchor the IBM hire to **1993** [[claim:CLM-2024-006]]. The same period overlaps with the public story of Medallion broadening into more **equities and statistical** sleeves [[claim:CLM-2024-002]]. **Nick Patterson** bridges genomics culture into hiring and scientific norms per Zuckerman [[claim:CLM-2026-014]].

### 2009–2025: Succession, opacity, modern scale claims

Executive succession to Brown/Mercer and Simons’s chairman role are documented at the **title** level [[claim:CLM-2026-013]]. Petabyte-scale warehouse language appears in modern profiles—treat as **journalistic characterization**, not verified engineering spec [[claim:CLM-2026-017]] [[claim:CLM-2026-018]]. For **performance** bands (Sharpe, leverage), see dedicated claims [[claim:CLM-2024-003]] [[claim:CLM-2024-004]]—we do not treat point estimates as facts.

## Open questions (public record gaps)

- Exact **legal entity** map across Limroy / Axcom / early Medallion feeder names (partially harmonized in Wikipedia, disputed elsewhere).
- **Month-level** PnL and gross vs net fee treatment for 1988–1990 “turnaround” years.
- Internal **org chart** under Straus vs Brown vs Mercer before 2009—titles are not workloads.
- **Options and volatility** sleeve timing and size (publicly under-documented) [[claim:CLM-2024-008]].
- Verifiable **hardware and data center** timeline inside East Setauket [[claim:CLM-2026-016]] [[claim:CLM-2026-018]].
- **Vendor vs proprietary** market data licenses across decades (cost and latency advantages are asserted in industry commentary but not itemized for Renaissance).
- **International expansion** of trading sleeves (Europe, Asia session futures) relative to Long Island operations center—mostly absent from accessible sources.
- **Prime broker** relationships and financing terms that would ground leverage narratives in contracts rather than journalism [[claim:CLM-2024-004]].

## Source methodology

We **prefer** [@zuckerman2019] for narrative sequencing where it aligns with multiple secondaries. [@wikipedia_renaissance_technologies] is used as a **concordance** table for dates that appear consistently across Bloomberg excerpts, Zuckerman, and fund-history essays—always with the caveat that Wikipedia can introduce smoothing errors [[claim:CLM-2024-006]]. [@berkeley_news_berlekamp2019] elevates specific claims about Berlekamp’s Axcom/Medallion episode toward **E2–E3** when it overlaps independent journalism. We avoid treating **tertiary blogs** as primary evidence for performance figures; where only blogs repeat a statistic, we omit it or downgrade to **E1** with an explicit hypothesis label elsewhere in the corpus [[claim:CLM-2024-003]].

When this chapter conflicts with older drafts in the repo (for example, a casual “1997 hire” date for Brown/Mercer), we treat **1993** as better supported in current public timelines and have corrected downstream tables [[claim:CLM-2024-006]]. Future edits should preserve **dated citations** in `data/bibliography.yaml` and bump `last_verified` when sources change.

## Notes on continuity

Public accounts suggest **continuous** strategy evolution rather than clean discrete regime switches [[claim:CLM-2024-002]]; the four-era narrative here is a **pedagogical** partition, not a claim of internal org milestones.
