# Phase VIII — Synthesis

**Last updated:** 2026-05-28

## Integrated framework

Public reconstruction supports a **layered** explanation: diversified short-horizon signals (Phase III) → neutralization and sizing (Phase V) → execution cost control (Phase IV) → organizational and capacity discipline (Phase VI). No layer alone matches reported Medallion metrics in our toys or in audited public filings.

## What plausibly explains performance

1. **Diversified short-horizon predictive signals** (stat arb, momentum, micro) with rigorous neutralization (SIG-001–007). See [core stat arb cluster](../chapters/03-signals.html#core-stat-arb).
2. **Risk stack** — vol targeting, banded leverage [[claim:CLM-2024-004]], portfolio diversification [[claim:CLM-2024-003]]. Gross Sharpe **point estimates above ~7** are treated as speculative upper range, not replicated facts.
3. **Execution quality** — reduces drag but unlikely sole driver [[claim:CLM-2024-007]]; EXP-04 bounds static impact costs.
4. **Options/volatility sleeves** — plausible but under-sourced [[claim:CLM-2024-008]] (SIG-008–010); see [options / vol cluster](../chapters/03-signals.html#options-vol).
5. **Organizational moat** — hiring, secrecy, long-horizon R&D [[claim:CLM-2024-009]]; peers share traits without identical outcomes.

## Performance metrics (ranges only)

| Quantity | Reconstruction stance |

## What remains unexplained

- Exact signal weights and model architectures (proprietary).
- Precise net Sharpe and leverage paths year-by-year.
- Full regulatory/tax optimization magnitude (E1 hypothesis below).
- Options sleeve share of PnL [[claim:CLM-2024-008]].

## Survivorship and failed peers

LTCM demonstrates concentration and leverage risks in relative-value structures [[claim:CLM-2024-010]]. Medallion’s shorter holding periods (inferred), diversification, and capacity caps differ materially — **survivorship bias** cautions against comparing only winners.

Other quant funds (DE Shaw spin-outs, crowded stat-arb) show talent and technology dispersion **without** Medallion-level flagship returns. Selection of one fund for study overweight success.

## Regulatory / structural edges (hypothesis)

We hypothesize (E1) incremental net benefits from:

- Tax-aware trading and wash-sale management
- Liquidity rebates and maker-taker optimization
- Preferential fee tiers at brokers (unverified for RenTech)

These cannot be quantified from public data; they belong in sensitivity analysis, not point estimates.

## Modern ML

Post-2010 NLP and alt-data (SIG-011–012) are **industry-wide**; RenTech’s early ML scale-up [[claim:CLM-2024-006]] suggests advantage in **integration and data hygiene**, not necessarily novel architectures alone. See [modern ML cluster](../chapters/03-signals.html#modern-ml).

## Simulation limits

Experiments under `experiments/` include costs and capacity but are **toys**. They support directional claims (e.g., costs erode naive Sharpe; ensemble sleeves can beat single sleeves under EXP-07) — not replication of Medallion returns.

## Counterarguments (expanded)

| Counterargument | Response in reconstruction |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Luck and selection** | One flagship among many strategies; external visibility biased toward Medallion |
| **Reporting bias** | LP anecdotes and books may overweight gross returns; net and fees opaque post-2005 [[claim:CLM-2026-008]] |
| **Capacity** | Past returns may not scale; fund closed to outsiders; returns not investable by public |
| **Crowding** | Stat-arb and micro edges decay as industry adopts similar tools |
| **Regime change** | 1989 drawdown [[claim:CLM-2026-005]] shows models fail without overhaul; past decades not guaranteed |
| **Data mining** | Large researcher count increases multiple-testing risk; secrecy prevents external audit |
| **Org not unique** | Peers hire PhDs and buy alt-data; org alone insufficient [[claim:CLM-2024-009]] |

## Verdict (epistemic)

The highest-fidelity **public** reconstruction is a **coherent plural explanation** with explicit evidence grades — not a single secret formula. Confidence is highest on **process** (scientific hiring, risk layering, capacity management) and moderate on **magnitude** of any one sleeve.
